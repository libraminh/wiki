Health and Safety

Health tip for the week: Watch for how you react to heat exposure

Not everyone reacts to heat in the same way. The way heat affects people varies from person to person and is influenced by:

  • Body weight (being overweight or obese can make it more difficult to cope with heat).
  • Age (particularly for people about 45 years and older).
  • Poor general health can make it difficult to cope with the heat.
  • A low level of fitness will make people more susceptible to feeling the extremes of heat.
  • Certain prescription drug use.
  • Medical conditions such as heart disease, high blood pressure, respiratory disease, pregnancy, etc. People with these medical conditions and some types of skin diseases and rashes may be more susceptible to heat.

Please take note of your individual circumstances and seek medical advice regarding your fitness for work/play in hot environments if you have predisposing medical conditions.
